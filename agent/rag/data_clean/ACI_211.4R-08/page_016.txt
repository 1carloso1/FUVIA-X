Further proportioning adjustment may be necessary. 7.2.3.1.7 Step 7: Conduct full-scale testing—There are always differences between proportions developed in the laboratory and those that result from concrete production, particularly in chemical admixture dosages. Making truck- sized batches of the concrete is the best way to confirm the required fresh and hardened properties. Full-scale testing is also an excellent way to confirm other properties that may be of particular interest to the project, such as finishability, shrinkage, temperature rise, and quality of the finished surface. It is also an opportunity for the entire production

7.3—Sample calculations 7.3.1. Introduction—High-strength concrete mixture proportioning is a more complex process than proportioning normal-strength concrete mixtures. In addition to silica fume, other supplementary cementitious materials may also be incorporated into the mixture as well as WRAs or
HRWRAs necessary to achieve the low w/cm needed to produce high-strength concrete.
