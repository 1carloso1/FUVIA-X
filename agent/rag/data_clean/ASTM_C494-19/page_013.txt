X3.5 Examples X3.5.1 Fig. X3.1 shows the infrared absorbance spectra for
two samples of a water-reducer based on calcium lignosul- fonate. The figure shows the typical patterns observed in these
types of spectra. A spectrum has absorption bands of varying intensities and widths. In addition, bands from different com-
ponents in the sample may overlap one another. At the higher
frequency (higher wavenumber) portion of the spectra in Fig.
X3.1, there are bands due to stretching of O-H bonds (the high
amplitude, broad bands) and stretching of C-H bonds (the less intense, narrow bands). The portion of the infrared spectrum
below a wavenumber of about 1800 cmTM! is known as the “fingerprint region” and typically contains many and some-
times overlapping bands. In this example, there is a good match in the locations and intensities of the absorption bands through-
out the spectra for both samples; the two samples would be
considered to be equivalent. X3.5.2 Fig. X3.2 shows infrared spectra of two samples of
